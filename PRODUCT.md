# Product

## Register

product

## Users

A single primary user: the owner, a self-directed retail stock investor and enthusiast who has ADHD and wants to invest more deliberately, with fewer impulsive, attention-driven decisions. Runs the app locally on a desktop / large monitor, glancing at it pre-market and through the trading day. Not a professional trader: a sharp amateur who wants public signals distilled into confident, explainable reads. Single-user personal tool, no multi-tenant onboarding.

## Product Purpose

A locally-run "invest smarter" cockpit that aggregates public signals and turns them into a small number of confident, explainable reads and next actions. The surface is deliberately narrowed to the five things the user actually acts on: Market Sentiment, Suggestions, News, Trades (insider filings), and Portfolio. Success is measured in seconds to answer: what is the market's mood, what should I look at today, what is happening in my names, what should I consider doing. Core principle: signals, not predictions. Every number shows its source and reasoning; unavailable sources show an honest error state, never fabricated or placeholder data.

## Brand Personality

Focused, precise, alive. A pro-grade trading terminal, not a consumer finance app. Voice is terse and direct, the way a good trading desk talks, never a chatbot. Three words: sharp, decisive, alive. It should make the user feel in command and calm under intensity: the surface is dense and vivid, but always ordered.

## Anti-references

Not a generic SaaS dashboard: no wall of identical cards, no gradient hero-metric tiles, no purple-gradient-on-everything, no "icon + heading + lorem" card grids. Not stuffy corporate finance (navy and gold, stock-photo trust). Not a hype or crypto-casino aesthetic. Not a chatty AI-assistant UI.

## Design Principles

1. Signals, not predictions. Every read shows its source; error and staleness are shown honestly, never faked. Practice what you preach.
2. One glance, one decision. Each screen answers a single question and points to the next action. Reduce choices instead of multiplying them. ADHD-first.
3. Structure carries intensity. A strict terminal grid and monospace numerics keep a maximal, vivid surface navigable instead of chaotic.
4. Motion means something. Live pulses, transitions, and animated data convey state changes and freshness, never decoration, and always respect reduce-motion.
5. Confident density. Show the real numbers, aligned and legible. Respect the user as capable; do not dumb it down.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

ADHD-first: strict visual grid so the eye always knows where to land, unmistakable current-state and focus indicators, generous hit targets, clear keyboard focus order, full keyboard navigation. Preserve the opt-in dyslexia mode (Atkinson Hyperlegible with looser spacing) and the reduce-motion setting; the maximal motion layer must fully collapse under `prefers-reduced-motion` and the in-app toggle. Maintain WCAG AA text and data contrast on the dark, saturated surface.
